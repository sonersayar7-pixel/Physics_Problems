### Step 1: The General Formula
The period of a simple pendulum is the time it takes to complete one full swing. It depends on the length of the pendulum and the acceleration due to gravity, as given by this formula:

$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

* $T$ = period of the pendulum
* $L$ = length of the string
* $g$ = acceleration due to gravity

### Step 2: Set up the Earth Equation
We are given that the pendulum has a period of 4 seconds on Earth. Let's call Earth's gravity $g_E$ and Earth's period $T_E$. We can write this as:

$$T_E = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g_E}} = 4$$

### Step 3: Define the Moon's Gravity
The problem states that the acceleration due to gravity on the Moon ($g_M$) is one-sixth of the gravity on Earth ($g_E$). 

$$g_M = \frac{g_E}{6}$$

### Step 4: Substitute into the Moon Equation
Now, we want to find the period on the Moon ($T_M$). We start with the base formula and substitute our value for $g_M$:

$$T_M = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g_M}}$$

Substitute the fraction for $g_M$:

$$T_M = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{\frac{g_E}{6}}}$$

### Step 5: Algebraic Simplification
When you divide by a fraction, it is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal. So, the $6$ moves up to the numerator:

$$T_M = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{6L}{g_E}}$$

Using the properties of square roots, we can separate the $6$ from the rest of the variables:

$$T_M = \sqrt{6} \cdot \left( 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g_E}} \right)$$

### Step 6: Substitute the Earth Period
Look closely at the equation above: the part inside the parentheses is the exact same formula we used for Earth's period in Step 2. Therefore, we can substitute $T_E$ for everything in the parentheses:

$$T_M = \sqrt{6} \cdot T_E$$

Since we know $T_E$ is 4 seconds, plug that in:

$$T_M = \sqrt{6} \cdot 4$$

### Step 7: Final Calculation
Now, calculate the final numerical value (using $\sqrt{6} \approx 2.449$):

$$T_M \approx 2.449 \cdot 4 \approx 9.796$$

**Conclusion:** The period of the pendulum on the Moon would be approximately **9.8 seconds**. Because gravity is weaker, the pendulum swings much slower.
