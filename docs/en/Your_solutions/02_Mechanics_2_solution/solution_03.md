### Step 1: Identify the given parameters from the equation
The general equation for simple harmonic motion is given by:

$$x(t) = A \cos(\omega t + \phi)$$

Where:
* $A$ is the amplitude (maximum displacement).
* $\omega$ is the angular frequency.
* $\phi$ is the phase constant.

Comparing this to our specific equation, $x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)$, we can extract:
* Mass ($m$) = $10 \text{ kg}$
* Amplitude ($A$) = $0.2 \text{ m}$
* Angular frequency ($\omega$) = $10\pi \text{ rad/s}$

---

### Step 2: Calculate the spring constant ($k$)
The angular frequency $\omega$ of a mass-spring system is related to the spring constant $k$ and the mass $m$ by the formula:

$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$$

To solve for $k$, we square both sides and multiply by $m$:

$$k = m \omega^2$$

Now, substitute the known values into the equation:

$$k = 10 \cdot (10\pi)^2$$
$$k = 10 \cdot 100\pi^2$$
$$k = 1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m}$$

*(Note: If a numerical approximation is needed, $\pi^2 \approx 9.87$, so $k \approx 9869.6 \text{ N/m}$.)*

---

### Step 3: Calculate the total mechanical energy ($E$)
In a simple harmonic oscillator, the total mechanical energy is conserved and is equal to the maximum potential energy stored in the spring. The formula is:

$$E = \frac{1}{2} k A^2$$

Substitute the values we found for $k$ and $A$:

$$E = \frac{1}{2} (1000\pi^2) \cdot (0.2)^2$$
$$E = 500\pi^2 \cdot 0.04$$
$$E = 20\pi^2 \text{ Joules}$$

*(Note: As a decimal approximation, $E \approx 20 \cdot 9.87 \approx 197.4 \text{ J}$.)*

**Final Answer:** The spring constant $k$ is **$1000\pi^2 \text{ N/m}$**, and the total mechanical energy of the system is **$20\pi^2 \text{ J}$**.
