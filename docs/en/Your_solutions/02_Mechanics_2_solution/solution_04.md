## 4. Energy & Momentum: Step-by-Step

Based on the equations provided, we can extract the initial values:
* **Height ($h$):** $3.0 \text{ m}$
* **Gravity ($g$):** $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$
* **Mass of object 1 ($m_1$):** $0.5 \text{ kg}$
* **Mass of object 2 ($m_2$):** $1.5 \text{ kg}$

---

### Step 1: Calculate the speed at the bottom ($v_1$)
To find the velocity of the first mass just before the collision, we use the principle of conservation of energy. The potential energy at the top is fully converted into kinetic energy at the bottom:

$$m_1gh = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2$$

We can cancel $m_1$ from both sides and rearrange the formula to solve for $v_1$:

$$v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}$$

Now, substitute the known values for gravity and height:

$$v_1 = \sqrt{2(9.8)(3.0)}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{58.8}$$
$$v_1 \approx 7.67 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### Step 2: Calculate velocity after the inelastic collision ($v_f$)
For a perfectly inelastic collision, the two masses stick together. We use the law of conservation of momentum, where the initial momentum of the system equals the final momentum:

$$m_1v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

Substitute the known masses and the velocity ($v_1$) we calculated in Step 1:

$$0.5 \cdot 7.67 = (0.5 + 1.5)v_f$$

Simplify both sides of the equation:

$$3.835 = 2v_f$$

Divide by 2 to isolate the final velocity ($v_f$):

$$v_f = \frac{3.835}{2}$$
$$v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}$$

**Final Answer:** The speed at the bottom before the collision is **$7.67 \text{ m/s}$**, and the final velocity of the combined masses after the collision is **$1.92 \text{ m/s}$**.
