## 5. Inelastic Collision: Step-by-Step

Based on the provided equation, we can identify the following initial values (assuming standard SI units of kilograms and meters per second):
* **Mass of object 1 ($m_r$):** 70 kg
* **Initial velocity of object 1 ($v_r$):** 3 m/s
* **Mass of object 2 ($m_c$):** 140 kg
* **Initial velocity of object 2:** 0 m/s (since it has no initial momentum)

---

### Step 1: Calculate the final velocity ($v_f$)
In a perfectly inelastic collision, the two objects stick together. According to the law of conservation of momentum, the total initial momentum equals the total final momentum:

$$m_r v_r = (m_r + m_c)v_f$$

Substitute the known values into the equation:

$$70 \cdot 3 = (70 + 140)v_f$$

Simplify both sides:

$$210 = 210v_f$$

Isolate $v_f$ to find the final velocity of the combined mass:

$$v_f = 1 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### Step 2: Calculate the initial kinetic energy ($KE_i$)
To determine if energy is conserved, we first need to find the total kinetic energy before the collision. Only the first object is moving, so we calculate its kinetic energy:

$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2}m_r v_r^2$$

Substitute the values:

$$KE_i = \frac{1}{2}(70)(3^2)$$
$$KE_i = 35 \cdot 9$$
$$KE_i = 315 \text{ J}$$

---

### Step 3: Calculate the final kinetic energy ($KE_f$)
Next, calculate the kinetic energy of the combined mass after the collision using the final velocity ($v_f$) we found in Step 1:

$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(m_r + m_c)v_f^2$$

Substitute the values:

$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(210)(1^2)$$
$$KE_f = \frac{1}{2}(210)(1)$$
$$KE_f = 105 \text{ J}$$

---

### Step 4: Conclusion on Energy Conservation
**Is energy conserved?** No. 

By comparing the kinetic energies, we can see that the system started with **315 J** but only had **105 J** after the collision. In perfectly inelastic collisions, kinetic energy is not conserved; the "lost" energy ($315 \text{ J} - 105 \text{ J} = 210 \text{ J}$) is dissipated into the environment as heat, sound, or the physical deformation of the objects.
