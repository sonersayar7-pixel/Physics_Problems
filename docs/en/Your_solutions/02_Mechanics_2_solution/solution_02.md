## 2. Harmonic Motion: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Compare with the general form
We are given the equation of motion for a $10 \text{ kg}$ mass:
$$x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)$$

By comparing this to the general form of simple harmonic motion, $x(t) = A \cos(\omega t)$, we can extract the specific values:
* **Amplitude ($A$):** $0.2 \text{ m}$
* **Angular Frequency ($\omega$):** $10\pi \text{ rad/s}$
* **Mass ($m$):** $10 \text{ kg}$

---

### Step 2: Find the Spring Constant ($k$)
The relationship between angular frequency, spring constant, and mass is defined as:
$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}$$

To solve for the spring constant ($k$), we rearrange the formula:
$$k = m\omega^2$$

Now, substitute the known values into the equation:
$$k = 10 \cdot (10\pi)^2$$
$$k = 10 \cdot 100\pi^2$$
$$k = 1000\pi^2 \approx 9870 \text{ N/m}$$
