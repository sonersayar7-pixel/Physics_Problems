## Section 8: Centripetal Acceleration at the Equator

**Given:**
* $R = 6,378,000 \text{ m}$
* $T = 86,400 \text{ s}$

### Mathematical Derivation
The angular velocity $\omega$ is:
$$\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} \approx 7.27 \times 10^{-5} \text{ rad/s}$$

The centripetal acceleration $a_c$ is:
$$a_c = \omega^2 R$$
$$a_c \approx (7.27 \times 10^{-5})^2 \cdot 6.378 \times 10^6$$
$$a_c \approx 0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2$$

| Property | Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Angular Velocity** | $7.27 \times 10^{-5} \text{ rad/s}$ |
| **Centripetal Accel.** | $0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2$ |
| **% of Standard Gravity** | $\approx 0.34\%$ |
